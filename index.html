Cyclistic Bike-Share: Detailed Case Study

Business Objective: Understand differences between casual riders and annual members to design conversion strategies.

Methodology

  • Analyzed 6 months of ride data (5M+ records)
  • Cleaned and transformed data using BigQuery
  • Created interactive dashboards in Power BI
  • Developed strategic recommendations

Key Insights

Usage Patterns

Casual riders take longer rides (~18 min) primarily on weekends, while members ride more frequently but shorter trips (~10 min) on weekdays.

Peak Times

Members show clear commute patterns (7-9AM, 5-7PM), while casual riders peak midday and weekends.

Bike Preferences

Casual riders prefer electric/docked bikes (58%) compared to members who favor classic bikes (72%).

Data Visualizations

Total rides by user type

Usage patterns by day of week

Hourly usage patterns

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Weekend membership trials create weekend and leisure-based promotions targeting casual riders.
  2. Commuter incentives promote short, efficient weekday rides to encourage commuting members.
  3. Station-based promotions run station-based ads in top casual rider locations.
  4. Bike-type specific offers offer conversion incentives(e.g free trial membership).

BigQuery Power BI DAX Excel

Financial Summary Report : Detailed Case Study

Business Objective: Visualize Revenue, Expenses, COS, and Depreciation using the Ending Balance of the month of MAY -TB 2025.

Methodology

  • Imported and cleaned the trial balance data in Excel to remove metadata and structure it for analysis.
  • Created a Category column to classify each item into Revenue, Expenses, COS, or Depreciation using logical Excel formulas.
  • Built a Dashboard using PowerBi to summarize the Closing Balance by financial category using SUM aggregation.
  • Visualized the results using Cards to compare category performance clearly.
  • Analyzed the outputs to generate insights, identify cost drivers, and support data-driven recommendations for stakeholders.

Key Insights

Revenue

Recorded the highest closing balance, indicating strong income generation.

Expenses

Followed closely, driven largely by staff costs, professional fees, and operational spending.

Cost of Sales

Represents a moderate share, reflecting the direct costs tied to revenue generation.

Depreciation

Shows a lower but consistent balance, reflecting asset aging and amortization of capital investments.

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Control Operational Expenses while revenue is strong, tightening expense categories such as staffing and third-party services will improve profitability.
  2. Optimize COS explore ways to reduce cost of sales through better supplier terms or process efficiencies.
  3. Invest Strategically given the depreciation levels, consider asset upgrades where necessary to maintain operational efficiency.
